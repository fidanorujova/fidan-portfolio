Hour of Code: Teaching Algorithmic Thinking

SITE 1101 – Principles of Information Systems

Team 52: Fidan Orujova (Team Leader), Nurgun Hajirahimova, Zahra Rufullayeva, Aliakbar Rzazade

Organized and conducted an Hour of Code (Hour of AI) event for the local community as part of a team-based university assignment. The project focused on teaching basic algorithmic thinking through interactive coding activities, making technology education accessible and engaging for community members. Our chosen community consisted of 7th-grade students at the Devechi Village Secondary School in the Shabran District.

Skills Gained

  • Teamwork & Collaboration
  • Communication & Presentation
  • Information Systems
  • Community Engagement

Logic Gates Hardware Project

SITE 1101 – Principles of Information Systems

Team 52: Fidan Orujova (Team Leader), Nurgun Hajirahimova, Zahra Rufullayeva, Aliakbar Rzazade

Constructed and tested fundamental logic gates (NOT, AND, OR) using transistors, resistors, LEDs, push buttons, and a solderless breadboard. This hands-on hardware project provided deep insights into how digital systems operate at the hardware level. We documented the construction process through video demonstrations with step-by-step explanations of each logic gate's operation. As a bonus challenge, we also constructed NAND and XOR gates in collaboration with other teams, requiring careful circuit design and strict adherence to laboratory safety protocols.

Skills Gained

  • Hardware Fundamentals
  • Logic Gate Design
  • Circuit Construction
  • Teamwork & Collaboration
  • Laboratory Safety
  • Video Documentation
